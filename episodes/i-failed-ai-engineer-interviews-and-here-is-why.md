# I Failed an AI Engineer Interview

## Episode Goal

Turn a painful interview failure into a practical roadmap for backend engineers who want to become AI engineers.

The viewer clicks because of the failure. They stay because the video explains the hidden gap and gives them a 30-day plan to avoid making the same mistake.

**Target length:** 12-14 minutes  
**Primary audience:** Backend engineers trying to move into AI engineering  
**Format:** Failure story + diagnosis + stair-step roadmap  
**Core tension:** Knowing how to call an LLM is not the same as knowing how to build a reliable AI product.

## One-Sentence Concept

I spent years building backend systems, but when I interviewed for an AI Engineer role, I discovered that my biggest weakness was not APIs or LangChain. It was proving that a model-powered system actually works.

## Packaging

### Recommended Title

**I Failed an AI Engineer Interview**

Why it works: It is short, personal, and immediately creates an open loop. The viewer wants to know why an experienced engineer failed and whether they would fail too.

### Ranked Alternative Titles

1. **I Failed an AI Engineer Interview**  
   Best CTR and most accurate. Let the thumbnail reveal the painful feedback.

2. **I Thought I Was Ready for AI Engineering. I Wasn't.**  
   Strong emotional curiosity, but slightly less specific.

3. **Why Backend Engineers Fail AI Interviews**  
   Best search-friendly option. Use if the channel is targeting a clearly technical audience.

4. **The AI Engineer Interview Exposed My Biggest Weakness**  
   Strong open loop, but less direct.

5. **I Failed My AI Engineer Interview - Then I Found the Gap**  
   Clear value proposition, but the title gives away more of the story.

### Thumbnail

Use only three dominant elements:

1. Left: your face, visibly shocked or defeated.
2. Center: a large red X over the words **AI ENGINEER INTERVIEW**.
3. Right: a whiteboard or screen showing:

```text
FAILED
NOT ML ENOUGH
```

Keep the background blurred: a Zoom interview screen, LeetCode-style editor, or LLM pipeline diagram can suggest context without competing for attention.

**Thumbnail text limit:** 3-4 words.  
**More aggressive variant:**

```text
I FAILED
AI ENGINEER
```

### Thumbnail Contract

The first three seconds must show the same failed-interview visual language: your reaction, the red X, and the exact feedback. Do not make the viewer wait for the reason.

## Retention Strategy

The story escalates through increasingly deeper gaps:

1. I thought AI engineering was backend engineering plus LLM APIs.
2. I could build features, but I could not prove they worked.
3. Reliable AI products require experimentation and ML fundamentals.
4. A generic chatbot demo is not strong evidence.
5. The solution is a measurable project with a scoreboard.

**Open loop:** What exactly was missing from my preparation?  
**Mid-video payoff:** The five-layer AI Engineer interview framework.  
**Final payoff:** A concrete 30-day plan and the project that will be built in the next episode.

#### Gap 1: I Could Build AI Features, But I Could Not Prove They Worked

**Host:**

> I could explain RAG. But could I design a reliable evaluation set? Could I measure hallucination rate? Could I compare retrieval quality? Could I prove that version B was actually better than version A?

**Visuals:**

- Highlight unanswered questions in red.

#### Gap 2: I Treated Prompts Like Code, Not Experiments

**Host:**

> In backend engineering, if the endpoint works, it works. In AI, output quality is probabilistic. The same prompt can work beautifully on 20 examples and fail horribly on the twenty-first.

**Visuals:**

- Show one prompt producing three outputs.

#### Gap 3: I Underestimated ML Fundamentals

#### Gap 4: I Did Not Have a Strong Enough Proof Project

**Host:**

> A chatbot demo is not enough anymore. Everyone has one. Better evidence is a system with evaluations, datasets, failure cases, monitoring, cost tracking, and a clear before-and-after improvement.

**3-minute re-engagement:** Transform the empty chatbot demo into a full evaluation dashboard. The audience should visually understand that the scoreboard is the difference.

### 10:00-12:30 - My New 30-Day Plan

**Host:**

> The goal is not to master all of AI in 30 days. The goal is to create interview-ready evidence that you can make AI systems better.

#### Week 1: Build the Baseline

- Pick one domain.
- Collect 100 real questions.
- Build a basic RAG pipeline.
- Log every answer.

#### Week 2: Build Evaluations

- Create golden answers.
- Measure retrieval hit rate.
- Measure answer correctness.
- Add LLM-as-judge scoring.
- Manually audit a sample of the results.

#### Week 3: Improve the System

- Add reranking.
- Experiment with chunking.
- Improve refusal behavior.
- Compare prompt variants.
- Optimize latency and cost.

#### Week 4: Package It Like an Engineer

- Write a technical blog post.
- Record a short demo.
- Show before-and-after metrics.
- Document failure cases.
- Prepare interview stories.

**Visuals:**

## Editing Notes

- Keep the first 30 seconds tightly scripted. No greeting, channel intro, or subscribe request.
- Use rapid cuts in the first two minutes, then let the five-layer framework breathe.
- Use a visual change every 5-10 seconds: face camera, diagram, screen recording, checklist, chart, or text hit.
- Keep technical explanations concrete. Every abstract idea should have a visual artifact.
- Repeat the evaluation dashboard throughout the episode so the final project feels inevitable.
- Do not overuse jargon. The audience should understand the gap even if they have not studied ML formally.
