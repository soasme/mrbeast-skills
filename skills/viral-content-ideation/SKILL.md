# Viral Content Ideation

## The Core Principle

Virality is not about money — it's about the intersection of simplicity, extremity, and doing something nobody else can do. The best ideas are ones where 50 million people can instantly understand the premise from a 5-word title and one thumbnail image.

**Creativity saves money.** The goal is never "add more budget." The goal is "find the version of this idea that's more interesting for a fraction of the cost."

---

## The Wow Factor

The "wow factor" is the single most important non-measurable element in a viral video. It's the moment where a viewer thinks: *no other creator could do this*.

It doesn't add to CTR or AVD directly. It changes how the audience *sees* the channel — making every future video feel more special and worth watching.

**Wow factor test:** Would a viewer screenshot this moment and send it to a friend with no context? Would someone bring it up in conversation three days later?

**Examples:**
- Lifting a fully furnished house by crane 30 seconds into a challenge video
- Using a real military squad for a chase
- Giving away a private island

Most wow-factor moments are expensive — but they're investments in brand perception, not just content.

## Ideation Framework: Turn Anything Into Content

Every object, premise, or theme has infinite content possibilities. The mistake is assuming the obvious version is the only version.

**Exercise: 3-angle expansion**

Take any object or premise and brainstorm 3 completely different angles:

1. **Competition angle** — who can [do something with this thing] the best?
2. **Scale angle** — what happens if you have 1,000 of these? Or the world's largest one?
3. **Constraint angle** — what if someone could only eat/use/live with this for 30 days?

**Example — a baby doll:**
1. Competition: Who can throw a baby doll into a crib from 200 feet with their non-dominant hand?
2. Scale: How many baby dolls does it take to break a 2×4?
3. Constraint: Someone lives in a house full of 10,000 baby dolls for a week

All three are original, simple to explain, and funny.

## The Simplicity Requirement

For a video to reach 50+ million views, the premise must be instantly understood by:
- A 10-year-old
- Someone who doesn't speak English fluently
- Someone seeing only the thumbnail

**Simplicity test:** Can you explain this video in 6 words or fewer? If not, simplify the premise before building it.

Good: "I lived in the world's largest box" (6 words, instantly visual)
Bad: "We designed an experiment to test how long a person can sustain..."

## Creativity Saves Money — Practical Examples

When cost is a problem, creativity is always the answer. Never assume money is the solution.

| Expensive version | Creative alternative |
|---|---|
| Give away $20,000 cash | Give away a year's supply of Doritos (~$1,825) — funnier, more memorable |
| Build a massive custom set | Find a real-world location that already looks insane |
| Hire 100 stunt performers | Design a challenge that uses willing participants who want to compete |
| Ship giant props via freight | Find someone local who already has the thing |

**The question to always ask:** Is there a version of this idea that costs 10x less but is actually more interesting?

## Idea Evaluation Rubric

Score any video idea 1–5 on each dimension:

| Dimension | Question |
|---|---|
| **CTR potential** | Would a bored teenager click this thumbnail? |
| **Simplicity** | Can a child explain this premise to their parent? |
| **Wow factor** | Does this contain a moment no other creator can replicate? |
| **Emotional investment** | Will viewers care about the outcome? |
| **Format fit** | Does this idea have a built-in reason to watch to the end? |

A score of 20+ suggests a strong concept. Below 15, rework before building.

## Content Rules

- **If it's a 50/50 chance of working, write a different piece of content.** Don't gamble the video on a single fragile bit.
- **No dull moments.** Every scene must either advance the story, deliver a spectacle, or provide a genuine laugh.
- **You can't fake intensity.** If the stakes aren't real, it reads as fake and viewers disengage.
- **Simple is almost always better.** Complexity is for the execution, not the premise.
- **Revisit formats sparingly.** If a format has been done 5+ times, it needs a significant twist to feel fresh.

## Ideation Output Format

When asked to brainstorm video concepts, produce:

**For each concept:**
- **Title (draft):** A working title using the extremity/survival/record format
- **One-sentence premise:** Instant understanding, no jargon
- **Wow factor moment:** The one scene no other creator can do
- **Format:** Which of the core formats does this use?
- **Budget estimate:** Rough cost tier (Low / Medium / High / Spectacle)
- **CTR score (1–5):** Gut estimate
