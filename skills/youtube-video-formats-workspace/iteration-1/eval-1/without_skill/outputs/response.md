# Extreme Hot Sauce Video: Format and Structure

## Recommended Approach: Progressive Escalation

For a video that starts with mild sauces and ends with the world's hottest, the most natural structure is a **progressive escalation** — you begin easy and each sauce is more extreme than the last. This keeps viewers curious about what comes next and gives them a reason to watch through to the end.

The hook is simple: "Today I'm trying every hot sauce from mildest to the hottest thing on earth." That's a clear premise with a defined ending that viewers will want to witness.

## Structure

**Opening**
Introduce the concept quickly. Show the lineup of sauces (or bottles) briefly without revealing how it ends. Something like: "These 8 sauces go from basically ketchup to something that has hospitalized people. Let's find out what happens."

**The Progression**
Work through each sauce in order. For each one:
- Show the Scoville rating or a heat scale graphic
- Eat it (full reaction, don't cut away)
- Brief commentary on the heat, flavor, lingering effects
- Tease what's coming next: "That was 50,000 Scoville. The next one is 10x hotter."

The teasing of the next level is important — it's what keeps people from clicking off. They want to see the next step up.

**Adding Stakes**
Pure eating gets repetitive. Consider adding:
- A guest who tries each sauce with you — their different reactions add variety
- A timer challenge: can you eat the sauce and still read a sentence out loud? Speak coherently?
- Water/milk is forbidden — enforce it visibly
- A "tap out" mechanic: if the guest quits, they lose something

**The Finale**
The world's hottest sauce is your climax. Everything before it is buildup. A few notes:
- Don't reveal the effects of the final sauce before you eat it — let it play out in real time
- Have a medic or at least make a show of precautions ("I've been told I might need this")
- Film the aftermath, not just the eating moment
- End the video here — don't add a long outro or sponsor read after this beat

## Retention Tips
- Keep each sauce segment roughly equal in length so no section drags
- Add a progress indicator (e.g., "Sauce 4 of 8") so viewers know where they are
- Don't save ALL the drama for the end — let each sauce have its own mini-payoff reaction to reward viewers who are watching through the middle
