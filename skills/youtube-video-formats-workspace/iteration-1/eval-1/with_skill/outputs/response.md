# Extreme Hot Sauce Video: Format Recommendation

## Format: Stair-Stepping (Progressive Escalation)

This concept is a textbook case for the **stair-stepping format**. You start small and escalate through the video — each sauce is hotter, more extreme, and more dramatic than the last. The viewer's logic becomes: "If the 5,000 Scoville sauce made him cry, I *have* to see what the 3,000,000 Scoville sauce does to him."

The format works because every transition raises a single question: *what happens at the next level?* That curiosity drives viewers to the end.

---

## Structure

### Opening (Hook)
Start with the mildest sauce — something almost anyone would eat without drama. Establish the format clearly: "We're going from the mildest hot sauce in the world all the way up to the hottest one ever made. Here's sauce #1."

Do NOT reveal the final sauce or show any footage from the finale in your hook. The payoff must be protected.

### Middle Segments (Escalation)
Each sauce segment needs more than just eating. Consider adding:

- **A reaction challenge**: Can you hold a conversation? Can you read a sentence? Can you stay in a hot tub? Each sauce adds a new physical constraint.
- **Guests or guests-at-risk**: Bring in a professional chef, a competitive eater, or a celebrity to try each sauce — the format then becomes "who taps out first?" layered on top of the escalation.
- **Visual heat indicators**: Display the Scoville unit count as a title card with each sauce — makes the escalation feel concrete and gameable for viewers.
- **Running commentary on what's ahead**: "That was 200,000 Scoville. We still have three more after this. One of them is over a million." This teases the payoff without revealing it.

Sample escalation structure:

| Beat | Sauce | Scoville Range | Moment |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Mild grocery-store hot sauce | ~450 | Easy, almost comedic |
| 2 | Medium commercial sauce | ~5,000 | Light discomfort, jokes |
| 3 | "Hot" wing sauce | ~50,000 | First real reaction |
| 4 | Ghost pepper sauce | ~1,000,000 | Visible distress, milk introduced |
| 5 | Carolina Reaper sauce | ~2,200,000 | Breakdown moment |
| 6 | World's hottest sauce | 3,000,000+ | Full payoff |

### The Finale (Payoff)
The world's hottest sauce is your payoff and it must be saved for the final beat. Never tease it visually or describe its effects until the moment it's consumed.

Strong payoff mechanics:
- Introduce the final sauce with clear stakes: "This is the hottest edible sauce ever created. Nobody on our team has tried it. I have a medic standing by."
- Capture the full unedited reaction — no cuts during the eating moment
- End the video immediately after the aftermath reaction. Do not add commentary or sponsor reads after the payoff.

---

## Critical Rule

Never place the world-record sauce anywhere but the very end. If you hint at what happens ("it was so bad I had to go to the hospital") before the finale, you've killed the format's retention power. The payoff only works if the outcome is unknown until it happens.

---

## Format Rotation Note

If you do a follow-up hot sauce video, do not repeat stair-stepping immediately. Use 2–3 different formats in between before returning to this structure. Viewers who've seen the escalation format already have a mental model of how it ends — the surprise is weaker on repetition.
