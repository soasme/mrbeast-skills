# Contractor Check-In: Giant Prop Castle

## Is This Okay?

No, this is not ideal. Going 5 days without any update from a contractor building a significant prop is a passive approach to production management. You've set a deadline and assumed that's enough — but with a prop this important, you need to stay more informed throughout the build process.

## What You Should Be Doing

### Follow Up Now

Reach out today to get a status update. Ask directly:
- Where are they in the build process?
- Are they on track to hit the deadline?
- Are there any issues with materials, scale, or timeline?

### Set Up Regular Check-Ins

Don't wait for the contractor to reach out to you. Set a schedule for regular updates — every 2-3 days at minimum. Ask for photos or short video clips of the work in progress so you can visually confirm the prop is developing as expected.

### Confirm the Details Are Right

Use the check-in to make sure the contractor's interpretation matches your vision: size, color, materials, structural requirements. It's much easier to correct course mid-build than after it's done.

### Plan for the Worst Case

What happens if the castle isn't ready? Think through contingencies:
- Can the shoot date shift if there's a delay?
- Is there a simpler version of the prop that could work in a pinch?
- Could you source a castle backdrop, rental prop, or alternative from another vendor?

### Communicate Your Real Deadline

If the shoot is in 3 weeks, consider asking for the prop to be complete 5-7 days before that. This gives you time to inspect it, make any changes, and arrange transportation without rushing.

## Bottom Line

The situation isn't a crisis yet, but it's heading in a risky direction. Proactive check-ins and visual confirmation of progress are how you catch problems early — before they become shoot-day emergencies. Reach out today.
