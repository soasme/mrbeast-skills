# Critical Components: World's Largest Swimming Pool Challenge

Here are the critical components for this production and how to track and protect each one.

## 1. The Olympic-Size Facility

The venue is the foundation of everything. Without a confirmed, accessible pool facility, nothing else matters.

**How to track it:**
- Get a signed contract as early as possible with clear dates and access times
- Confirm the booking 1 week before and again 48 hours before shoot
- Identify a backup facility in case of cancellation
- Make sure the contract covers all the hours you need for setup, filming, and breakdown

## 2. Custom Floating Obstacles

These are likely central to the video concept and thumbnail. They need to be built, tested, and delivered on time.

**How to track it:**
- Establish clear milestones with the contractor (design approval, build start, midpoint review, delivery)
- Request progress photos or updates at each milestone
- Test the obstacles in water before shoot day if at all possible
- Plan for transportation to the facility and confirm delivery timing
- Have a contingency if they arrive damaged — can you source replacements or simplify the design?

## 3. The 20 Contestants

People are unpredictable. Getting 20 people to show up reliably on shoot day requires active management.

**How to track it:**
- Recruit 25-30 people to guarantee 20 show up
- Collect contact information and send reminders at 1 week, 3 days, and 1 day before shoot
- Have alternates ready to fill spots if people cancel last minute
- Brief contestants on logistics: location, time, what to wear, what to expect

## 4. Camera and Production Equipment

Water environments are hard on equipment. The right gear needs to be confirmed and operational.

**How to track it:**
- Confirm all equipment rentals in advance and pick up a day early if possible
- Ensure underwater or waterproof camera options are available
- Have backup batteries, memory cards, and essential gear on hand
- Test all equipment before shoot day

## 5. Safety and Liability Clearances

A pool production with 20 contestants requires lifeguards, waivers, and potentially permits.

**How to track it:**
- Hire certified lifeguards and confirm their availability well in advance
- Collect signed liability waivers from all contestants before shoot day
- Confirm any permits required by the facility or local regulations
- Have a clear safety protocol and brief everyone on it

## Summary

The three highest-risk components are the facility, the floating obstacles, and the contestants — because any one of them falling through could make the video impossible to film. Equipment and safety logistics are important but more easily substituted or solved day-of. Focus your energy and check-ins most heavily on those top three.
