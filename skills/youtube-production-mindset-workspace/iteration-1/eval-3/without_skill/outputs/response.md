# Shoot Day Crisis: Pyrotechnics Won't Work

With 4 hours left in the shoot, here's how to handle this situation.

## Step 1: Assess the Actual Problem

Get clarity on what's wrong immediately. Is it:
- A technical malfunction that might be fixable?
- A missing component or fuel issue?
- A safety concern that prevents use entirely?

Understanding the root cause tells you which path forward makes the most sense.

## Step 2: Work on a Fix

If there's any chance the pyrotechnics can be repaired or resolved in 1-2 hours, assign the FX team to continue troubleshooting while you simultaneously work on alternatives. Don't put all your eggs in one basket.

## Step 3: Find Alternatives

While the FX team works on the original problem, start exploring alternatives:

- **Call other FX vendors or rental companies** — can someone get equipment or a technician to the location in time?
- **Look for practical alternatives** — are there simpler effects (smoke, confetti, colored powder, sparklers) that could stand in for the pyrotechnics visually?
- **Reframe the shot** — can the scene be filmed in a way that creates a strong visual impact without pyrotechnics? Camera angles, editing tricks, and sound design can often compensate for missing effects in post.

## Step 4: Decide and Commit

At some point — probably within the next 60-90 minutes — you need to make a decision and commit to it. Don't spend all 4 hours troubleshooting only to have nothing to show for it. Set a decision deadline: "If we don't have a working pyro solution by 2 hours from now, we pivot to Plan B."

## Step 5: Communicate with the Director/Creator

Make sure whoever is creatively leading the production knows what's happening and is part of the decision. They may be able to adjust the vision for the finale scene to work with available resources, or they may have contacts or ideas you don't.

## Step 6: Film What You Can

Whatever direction you go, make sure it gets captured well. If you're pivoting to a simpler effect, give it the best framing and energy possible. If you end up skipping the planned finale, capture the best alternative ending available.

## Key Point

Four hours is enough time to adapt if you act quickly and make decisions decisively. The worst outcome is spending all of it troubleshooting the original problem and ending up with nothing.
