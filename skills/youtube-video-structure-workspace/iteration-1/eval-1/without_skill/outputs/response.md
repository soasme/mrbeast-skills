# Video Structure: "10 People Compete to Live in a Glass Box for 30 Days — Last One Stays Wins $500,000"

**Target length: 15 minutes**

---

## Minute 0–2: Introduction

Open with a wide establishing shot of the glass box challenge set. Host explains the rules: 10 people, 30 days, last person inside wins $500,000. Introduce the 10 contestants with brief name cards and one-sentence bios. Show them entering the box together.

---

## Minute 2–4: Day 1 Setup

Contestants settle in. Show them figuring out sleeping arrangements, establishing routines, and getting to know each other. First conversations — who's strategizing, who's there for fun, who's already homesick.

---

## Minute 4–7: Early Days (Days 1–7)

Cover the first week in montage form. Show the first conflicts over space and noise. Someone gets voted into a disadvantage position. First sign of alliances forming. Day 5 or 6 — first person seriously considers quitting but stays.

---

## Minute 7–10: Mid-Game Drama (Days 8–20)

The real game begins. Show the social dynamics — alliances, betrayals, and power plays. Someone quits unexpectedly around day 10, dropping to 9 contestants. A dramatic argument at day 15. The mental strain becomes visible on everyone's face. Show a fun, lighter moment — contestants singing or playing games — for contrast.

---

## Minute 10–12: Final Stretch (Days 21–28)

Final 3–4 contestants remain. The prize feels real and close. Contestants start making deals. Show the last emotional conversations — people missing their families, questioning whether it's worth it.

---

## Minute 12–14: Final Days and Decision

Day 28–30. The final tension build. Who will stay until the very end? Show the last contestant left standing. Host enters the box for the first time. Official announcement.

---

## Minute 14–15: Winner Reveal and Outro

Winner is announced. Confetti, celebration. Winner's emotional reaction. Quick clips of all the contestants reflecting on the experience. Host closes out the video with a call to action: "Subscribe for more challenges like this!"

---

## Summary

The 15-minute structure covers the full arc from setup to payoff, with the mid-game section (minutes 7–10) providing the main drama and the back half (minutes 10–15) serving as the emotional resolution.
