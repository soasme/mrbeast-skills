# Video Structure: "10 People Compete to Live in a Glass Box for 30 Days — Last One Stays Wins $500,000"

**Target length: 15 minutes**

---

## TITLE/THUMBNAIL EXPECTATION
Viewer clicks expecting to see: a glass box with 10 people inside it, a $500,000 prize, and the promise of 30 days of competition.

---

## HOOK (0–60 seconds)

**Opening visual (confirms thumbnail):**
Open on a wide drone shot of the glass box already constructed with 10 people visible inside it. Immediately cut to a close-up of the $500,000 cash stacked on a table outside the box. The first line of narration: "These 10 people just agreed to live in this glass box for 30 days. The last one inside wins half a million dollars."

**Most exciting front-loaded content:**
Show a rapid-fire montage of what's to come — someone breaking down crying, someone getting medically evacuated, an argument erupting, and the final contestant standing alone in the box holding the check. (Teaser cut, 8–10 seconds.)

**Stakes established:**
Back to Day 1. Host explains: one person leaves the box = they're eliminated forever. Last one remaining wins $500,000. No more setup needed — cut immediately to the door sealing shut.

---

## TRANSITION (1–3 minutes)

**Execution begins:**
Day 1 begins. Show the contestants immediately realizing the psychological reality: they're trapped together with strangers, they can't leave to use a private bathroom, and there are no beds — just the floor.

**Crazy progression (compress early days):**
Don't linger on Day 1 linearly. Compress:
- Day 1 → First argument over sleeping positions
- Day 2 → First person considers quitting (close-up of them at the glass wall, staring out)
- Day 3 → First elimination: someone taps out at 3am, box drops to 9 people. The exit is played with fanfare — confetti, crowd cheers — while the remaining 9 are silent.

This gives viewers the emotional arc of 3 days in under 2 minutes.

**3-Minute Re-engagement:**
At exactly the 3-minute mark: the production crew backs a flatbed truck up to the box and uses a crane to lower a second, smaller "penalty box" — a 4x4 cage — onto the roof of the glass box. The host announces: "Every day, the last person voted least-likely-to-win will spend 24 hours up there." One contestant is immediately sent up. The other 9 watch from below in horror. This is visually spectacular, requires zero explanation, and raises the stakes of the game instantly.

---

## STORY BUILD (3–6 minutes)

**Scene 1 (3:00–4:00):**
Introduce the 2–3 most interesting contestants through action, not interview. The strategist who immediately starts making alliances. The wildcard who brought a guitar and won't stop playing it. The quiet one in the corner who no one's noticed yet.

**Scene 2 (4:00–5:00):**
Day 5 — first real alliance forms on camera. Two contestants shake hands over a deal: "We don't vote each other into the penalty box." Quick scene changes: overhead shot of the box at night, hands knocking on glass from inside, someone sleeping standing up.

**Scene 3 (5:00–6:00):**
Days 6–8: Two more eliminations in rapid succession. Box is now down to 7. Show the remaining contestants starting to form tribal loyalty. The guitar player is beloved. Someone is clearly emerging as a villain — they've gotten two people eliminated through manipulation.

**6-Minute Re-engagement:**
At minute 6: the host walks up and announces that starting now, the box's AC unit will be turned off. Temperature inside will reach 95°F. The contestants have a group meltdown — visible sweat, someone immediately starts removing layers, two people huddle near the one remaining vent. This is story-critical (raises stakes for the rest of the video and explains who mentally breaks next) and takes about 45 seconds to set up.

---

## BACK HALF (6 minutes – 15 minutes)

**Lull dynamic in play:**
Viewers who've made it to 6 minutes are now committed. The back half can breathe more — longer scenes, slower emotional moments, and unplanned content can be woven in.

**Main story beats (6–11 min):**
- Day 10 (6:30): The villain successfully gets the fan-favorite (guitar player) eliminated. The remaining 6 turn on the villain. Great dramatic scene, plays out over 2 minutes.
- Day 14 (8:30): Midpoint — a medical check-in reveals one contestant has a fever. Medical team enters the box (breaking the sealed environment for the first time). This is inherently dramatic. They don't leave, but viewers see a close call.
- Day 18 (10:00): The villain is finally voted into the penalty box. Crowd outside goes wild. The remaining 5 celebrate. Real character development — the villain's composure breaks for the first time.
- Day 22–25 (11:00–12:30): The back half lull. Genuine friendship forming between the final 3. Slow montage of them laughing, sharing food through the glass slot, sleeping in shifts. This is the emotional reward for viewers who stayed.

**Unexpected/unplanned content opportunity (12:30–13:30):**
During the final stretch, a massive rainstorm hits. The glass box floods slightly from a drainage failure. Production scrambles on camera. The final 3 are standing on top of their bags, laughing and miserable. This is unscripted gold — play it straight, don't cut it.

**Final payoff (13:30–14:30):**
Day 30. Final 3 remain. Host calls them to the door. Two must exit simultaneously, leaving one winner. Show the negotiation: the two with the weakest claims (by money need or previous deal) agree to leave together. One contestant walks out into the $500,000. The check is presented. Confetti. The winner's reaction is the full emotional payoff — crying, jumping, calling family.

**Ending (14:30–15:00):**
The winner's first phone call to their family, played through the glass box speaker. 30 seconds. Then cut to black immediately after they say "I won." No outro. No "like and subscribe." Hard cut.

| 1–3 | Day 1–3 compression, first elimination | HIGH | Crazy progression |
| 3 | Penalty box crane drop | MAX SPECTACLE | 3-min re-engagement |
| 3–6 | Character intros, alliances, 2 more eliminations | HIGH/VARIED | Story investment |
| 6 | AC turned off announcement | HIGH SPECTACLE | 6-min re-engagement |

| 13:30–14:30 | Day 30 winner decision + reveal | MAX | Final payoff |

---

## STRUCTURAL NOTES

- The thumbnail visual (glass box + people inside) appears in the first 5 seconds — no viewer feels deceived.
- No rule explanation monologue — rules are understood through action by minute 1.
- Both re-engagements (crane at 3 min, heat wave at 6 min) are story-integrated, not random spectacle.
- The back half avoids signaling the end until Day 30 is explicitly announced at 13:30.
- Ending is abrupt: winner's phone call ends the video. No transition to outro.
