# Video Idea Evaluation

## Idea A: "I trained for 1 year with an Olympic swimming coach and then competed against the coach"

### Scoring

| Dimension | Score | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| CTR potential | 3 | Interesting premise but needs a compelling thumbnail. "Person vs. their own coach" is relatable. |
| Simplicity | 4 | Easy to understand — person trains hard, faces their trainer. |
| Wow factor | 3 | The competition itself is exciting, but it's similar to other training challenge videos. |
| Emotional investment | 4 | Viewers root for the underdog creator. Personal journey makes this compelling. |
| Format fit | 4 | Natural arc: training journey, climax of competition, resolution. |
| **Total** | **18/25** | |

---

## Idea B: "I built a swimming pool out of 50,000 water balloons"

### Scoring

| Dimension | Score | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| CTR potential | 5 | Visually striking thumbnail — a pool of colorful water balloons is immediately eye-catching. |
| Simplicity | 5 | Instantly understood — balloons forming a pool. |
| Wow factor | 4 | The visual of actually swimming in 50,000 balloons is memorable and unique. |
| Emotional investment | 2 | Less personal stakes. It's more of a spectacle than a journey. |
| Format fit | 3 | Great for a short video but may struggle to hold attention over 15+ minutes. |
| **Total** | **19/25** | |

---

## Recommendation

**Produce Idea B.**

Idea B scores higher overall and has stronger immediate appeal (CTR, simplicity, wow factor). The visual concept is stronger for thumbnails and short clips.

However, Idea B should add some challenge or stakes to boost emotional investment — for example, "Can I actually swim through 50,000 water balloons?" or inviting friends to compete.

**Improving Idea A:** To make Idea A stronger, emphasize the personal narrative more and add clearer visual moments for the thumbnail — show the training montage highlights and the dramatic face-off moment.
