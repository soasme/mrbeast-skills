# Two Brand Deals in a 12-Minute Obstacle Course Competition Video

## Placement Strategy

For a 12-minute video with 20 contestants competing for $500,000, here's how I'd think about placement:

**Option 1: One sponsor early, one sponsor late**
- First brand deal around **minutes 2–3** (after the intro but before the action fully kicks off)
- Second brand deal around **minutes 9–10** (near the end, when you're building to the finale)

**Option 2: Both in the back half**
- First brand deal around **minutes 6–7**
- Second brand deal around **minutes 10–11**

Option 2 is probably safer since viewers are more engaged by then and less likely to click away.

## Best Integration Types for This Format

Given the obstacle course competition theme, these types of brands/integrations work well:

- **Energy drinks or sports nutrition** — contestants can be seen using them, feels natural in a physical competition
- **Athletic apparel or gear** — can be integrated as prizes or used during the competition
- **Mobile games** — can create a side challenge where contestants compete in the game for extra money
- **Tech/wearables** — could track performance during obstacles

For each integration, try to make the brand part of the action rather than stopping everything for an ad. For example, if it's an energy drink, show contestants grabbing drinks between rounds.

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

1. **Doing both ads back-to-back** — that creates a long dead zone in the middle of the video
2. **Sponsors that feel totally unrelated** to physical competition (like a financial services ad)
3. **Reading off a script** — keep it natural and conversational
4. **Going too long** — each segment should be 30–60 seconds max
5. **Putting sponsors before the competition starts** — viewers tune in for the competition, not ads

## General Tips

- Use different formats for each sponsor to keep it fresh (one could be a product demo, one could be comedic)
- Make sure the contest/competition keeps moving — sponsors shouldn't feel like they stop the action completely
- Have good transitions in and out of sponsor segments
