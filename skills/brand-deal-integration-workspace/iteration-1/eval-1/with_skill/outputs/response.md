# Brand Deal Integration: HydroMax in "I Survived 7 Days in the Sahara Desert"

## Placement Recommendation

**Place the HydroMax segment at the 7–8 minute mark** (in a 13-minute video, this falls squarely in the back half, after the second re-engagement beat).

The first 6 minutes should be all story — the setup, the first crisis, the survival stakes. By minute 7, the viewer is invested and has already seen enough to commit to finishing the video. The brief pace change here is tolerable because they've already got skin in the game.

Do **not** place it in the first 60 seconds (destroys the hook), and avoid the 1–6 minute window (interrupts story build). The desert survival format lends itself to a natural "rest moment" midway through — use that beat.

---

## Why HydroMax Is a Perfect Thematic Fit

Dehydration is literally the central threat in a Sahara survival video. This is not a forced integration — this is the ideal sponsor. HydroMax doesn't need to be explained; the context sells it. Lean into that.

---

## Segment Script / Plan

**Integration type:** Story-tied + physical demonstration

**Who delivers it:** Host, on camera, in the desert environment (not in a cutaway studio shot)

**Runtime target:** 45 seconds

---

### Visual Setup

The host is sitting in whatever shelter they've constructed — sweating, clearly depleted. They look directly into the camera.

---

### Script (conversational, not read from a script)

> "Okay — real talk for a second. Day three. I have not had a full night of sleep. I've been rationing water. And honestly? The thing that's kept me functional is this."

*[Holds up HydroMax packet, tears it open, mixes it into water bottle on camera]*

> "HydroMax. These are electrolyte packets — you mix one into your water and it actually does what sports drinks claim to do. I've been going through two of these a day out here, and I genuinely think I'd be in rough shape without them."

*[Takes a drink]*

> "If you want to try them, I'll put a link in the description — use code BEAST for 25% off your first order."

*[Beat, then:]*

> "Alright. Back to trying not to die."

---

## Segment Review Checklist

- [x] Entertaining enough to watch without skip option — the setting (actual desert, depleted host) makes the product feel real
- [x] Connects to the video's story — dehydration/electrolytes is the central survival theme
- [x] Does not sound like a script — conversational, first-person, admits real struggle
- [x] Has a visual element — physical demonstration (opening packet, mixing, drinking)
- [x] CTA is simple and memorable — "link in description, code BEAST for 25% off"
- [x] Product is accurately represented — electrolyte supplementation is a legitimate use case
- [x] Placed after the 6-minute mark — recommended at 7–8 minutes

---

## What to Avoid

- Do **not** open with "This video is brought to you by HydroMax" — jump straight into the survival moment
- Do **not** list product features or ingredient counts — that's marketing copy, not content
- Do **not** film this as a clean, well-lit studio insert — keep the visual consistent with the desert footage; the roughness is what makes it believable
- Do **not** run longer than 60 seconds — the segment works because it's tight
