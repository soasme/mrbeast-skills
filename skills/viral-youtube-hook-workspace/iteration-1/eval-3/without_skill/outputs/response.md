# "World's Largest Slip N Slide" — Hook to Minutes 1–3 Plan

## Opening Hook (0–60 sec)

Start with an establishing drone shot of the entire 500-foot slide to confirm the scale the thumbnail promised. The creator is at the top of the slide, talking to camera:

> "This is 500 feet of pure chaos. Today, we're finding out if anyone can survive the whole ride — and the last person standing wins $50,000."

Show a quick test run so viewers see someone actually going down the slide in the first 30 seconds. Establish the rules briefly: stay on the slide, hit the landing zone, last person to be eliminated wins.

## Minutes 1–3 Plan

### Minute 1: First Contestant Runs

Get the competition started immediately. Show the first 3–4 contestants going down the slide. Keep cuts quick — each run gets about 10–15 seconds of screen time. Include at least one wipeout in the first minute to show the stakes are real. Show crowd reactions.

### Minute 1:30: First Elimination Drama

Show a close call and then a definitive elimination with a dramatic slow-motion wipeout. This builds emotional investment — viewers start picking favorites and worrying about specific people.

### Minute 2: Character Introductions

Briefly introduce 2–3 contestants who seem like they'll be important to the story — the favorite, the underdog, maybe someone funny or charismatic. These 5-second clips give viewers people to root for.

### Minute 2:30–3:00: Raise the Stakes

Reveal a twist or escalation — maybe the water pressure increases, obstacles are added, or the slide gets slippery. This gives the viewer a reason to keep watching: "Oh, it's going to get even harder." End this section on a moment of anticipation — someone at the top of the slide looking nervous as the new conditions are explained.

By minute 3, viewers should understand who the key characters are, have seen real competition and elimination, and be excited about what's coming next in the video.
