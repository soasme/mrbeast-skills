# First 60 Seconds: "I Survived 100 Hours Buried Underground"

Here's how I'd write the opening of this video:

## Opening (0–15 seconds)

The video starts with the creator standing outside next to a freshly dug hole in the ground. They speak directly to camera:

> "Hey guys! What is up, today's video is absolutely insane. I am literally about to go underground for 100 hours. That's over four days — buried alive, basically. We've been planning this for weeks and I cannot believe we're actually doing this."

Cut to showing the chamber: a ladder going down, the underground space visible from above.

## Descent (15–30 seconds)

The creator climbs down the ladder as camera crews film from above and below.

> "So the way this works — I'm going down into this chamber. The team is going to seal it up. I have food, water, and a flashlight. 100 hours. No cheating, no leaving early."

They reach the bottom. Camera shows the small dirt chamber — dirt walls, low ceiling, flashlight on a hook.

> "This is where I'll be living. Is this crazy? Yes. Are we doing it anyway? Also yes."

## Setup (30–50 seconds)

Creator shows what they've brought down:

> "I've got enough food for about five days, water — we calculated this very carefully — and I've got my camera equipment so you guys can watch everything that happens down here. There's also a small vent for air."

They gesture around the small space, showing the dimensions.

> "It's not huge. But it's gonna be home for the next hundred hours."

## Hook close (50–60 seconds)

Creator looks directly at camera, flashlight in hand — matching the thumbnail image.

> "Alright. Let's seal this thing up. The clock starts now."

Cut to outside: the wooden hatch closes. We hear it latch. A clock graphic appears: **100:00:00**. Countdown starts.
