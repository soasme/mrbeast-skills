# First 60 Seconds: "I Survived 100 Hours Buried Underground"

## Pre-Script Checklist

- **Thumbnail promise**: Creator in a small dirt chamber with a flashlight — underground, confined, dim light from flashlight.
- **Title promise**: 100 hours buried underground.
- **First 15 seconds confirmation**: Must show the underground chamber with the creator inside it, flashlight in hand.

---

## Opening Beat (0–15 sec)

**What is seen:**
The video opens MID-ACTION — not before the burial, not at setup. The creator is already inside the underground chamber. The shot is tight: dirt walls on all sides, low ceiling, the flashlight casting dramatic shadows. We see the creator's face illuminated by the flashlight, expression somewhere between determined and uncomfortable. No drone flyover. No title card montage. The chamber, immediately.

**What is said (pre-planned, no improv):**
> "I'm already underground. This chamber is roughly 8 feet by 6 feet — and I have to survive here for 100 hours."

*[The creator pans the flashlight slowly around the chamber — dirt walls, a small cot, a sealed water jug, and a clock on the wall showing Hour 0:00.]*

> "No windows. No natural light. The only way out… is when that clock hits zero."

**Why this works:** The viewer clicked for the underground chamber from the thumbnail. They see it within 2 seconds. The creator confirms the premise in one sentence without filler.

---

## Hook Sequence (15–45 sec)

**What is seen:**
Quick cuts begin — the tight space from multiple angles to emphasize how confined it is. The creator tries to stand fully upright and can't quite. They knock a hand against the dirt wall. A split-second cut to the outside: a sealed wooden hatch being closed over the hole, a padlock clicking shut, and then darkness except for the flashlight.

Clock graphic: **100:00:00** — countdown begins. Timestamped in the corner.

**What is said:**
> "Four days. A hundred hours. The rules: I cannot break the surface. No phone signal down here. One box of food, two gallons of water — calculated exactly. If I run out early, that's on me."

*[Creator holds up the food box — visible and clearly limited. Sets it down.]*

> "The hardest part isn't the dark. The first hour, everyone said the same thing — it's the silence."

*[Dead silence on the audio for 3 full seconds. Just the faint sound of the creator breathing.]*

**Why this works:** The silence beat is visceral — the viewer feels the isolation. We've already seen the space, established the stakes, and created a sensory hook without over-explaining.

---

## Premise Lock (45–60 sec)

**What is seen:**
Creator sits down on the cot. Close-up on their face, lit by flashlight. Composed, but aware.

**What is said:**
> "Every 10 hours, I get a check-in. A phone goes down through a tube in the wall — one minute to talk, then it goes back up. That's my only connection to the surface."

*[Creator gestures to a small PVC pipe in the corner of the chamber — barely visible. It's real and it's sparse.]*

> "Hour one. Ninety-nine to go. Let's see what happens to your mind when there's nothing but dirt around you."

*[Clock on the wall. 99:47:00. Ticking.]*

**Why this works:** Stakes are locked in. The rules are simple, specific, and done in one breath. No lengthy explanation. The viewer now understands exactly what they're about to watch — and they're already wondering if the creator will make it.

---

## Transition Signal (60–90 sec)

**What is seen:**
Time-lapse begins. The clock on the wall advances visibly. The creator goes through early-hour motions: eating, trying to sleep, staring at the ceiling. We compress hours 1 through 12 into 25 seconds of footage — not narrated, just music and visuals. Multiple emotional states visible: boredom, restlessness, attempted distraction.

At the 90-second mark, the creator is awake at what the clock shows as Hour 14. They look directly at camera:

**What is said:**
> "Something is starting to feel wrong."

*[Hard cut to black.]*

**Why this works:** The transition stops explaining and starts executing. The viewer is already 14 hours in with the creator, emotionally invested before the 2-minute mark. The "something is starting to feel wrong" line re-engages them and propels them into the next section.

---

## Summary of First-60-Seconds Checklist Compliance

- [x] Thumbnail/title promise (underground chamber with flashlight) visible within first 2 seconds
- [x] Good lighting — flashlight creates dramatic, intentional contrast; not muddy or undefined
- [x] No slow warmup — drops viewer directly into the chamber, mid-situation
- [x] Every word pre-planned — no "so basically", no "uh", no "welcome back"
- [x] "This is what I clicked for" confirmed by 10 seconds
- [x] No "in today's video we're going to..." language
- [x] Stakes and rules established by second 55, clean and specific
